Historical Places

Cairo Attractions draw more tourists to Egypt than any other part of the country. With the mind numbing Giza pyramids on its doorstep, and the pyramids of Saqqara a stone throw away, it’s easy to see why.


Historical Places

The Pyramids and Sphinx

Giza

Price: Starting from E£ 450

The most famous landmarks in Egypt and part of the seven wounders of the world. The Pyramids and the Sphinx are a must visit.

Cairo Citadel

Al Abageyah, El-Khalifa

Price:E£ 393

Also Known as Salah El Din citadel built in 1176.Located near the center of Cairo,it is now a preserved historic site, including mosques and museums.

Cairo Tower

3 Mari Gerges, Kom Ghorab, Old Cairo

Price: E£ 25

The second most famous landmark in egypt after the pyramids, standing at 187 meters tall known to locals as Nassers Pineapple the tower oversees all of Cairo.

Khan El-Khalili

El-Gamaleya, El Gamaliya

Price: Starting from E£ 0

A famous bazar and souq in the historical center of cairo. Is one of the main attractions in Cairo for both Egyptians and tourists alike.

Al Moez Street

Al moez street, Old Cairo

Price: Starting from E£ 0

Located right next to Khan El-Khalili, al moez street is the perfect place for toursit want to experience historic islamic Cairo as the street dates back to the 10th century.

Al-Azhar Mosque

El-Darb El-Ahmar, Cairo

Price: Free

One of the worlds most famous and visited mosques.Built in 970 in the newely established capital at the time. Was the start of Al-azhar university>

Abdeen palace

El-Gomhoreya Square, Rahbet Abdin

Price: E£ 20

One of the oldest museums in Cairo buit in 1874 by Khedive Ismail.Contains expensive artwork and is mostly decorated with gold.

Saqqara Pyramid

Al Giza Desert

Price: Starting from E£ 60

The saqqara step pyramid built in the 27th century BC is considered to be the oldest coloasal stone building in Egypt.Located in Memphis the capital of Ancient Egypt.

Mosque of Ibn Tulun

Tolon, El-Sayeda Zainab

Price: E£ Free

The oldest mosque in Egypt and Africa,built in 878 and named after the ruler at the time Ahmad Ibn Tulun.A sample of beautiful Egyptian architecture.
