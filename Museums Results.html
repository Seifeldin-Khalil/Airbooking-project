Museums

From rich Islamic and Coptic history to the ancient Egyptians, Egypt has one of the richest cultures in the world. As the capital, Cairo is home to some great museums that protect and showcase Egypt’s unique history. Here are a few that shouldn’t be missed.


Museums

Egyptian Museum

Tahrir Square rd, Tahrir Square

Price:Starting from E£ 80

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo is home to over 120,000 pieces of Anicient Egptian antiques. Designed by French architet Marcel Dourgnon and build in 1901.

Museum of Islamic Art

Port Said, St El-Darb El-Ahmar

Price:Starts from E£ 5

Considered to be one of the best mueseums of its kind. Home to over 4,500 pices of artwork from all periods of islamic history.

Coptic Museum

3 Mari Gerges, Kom Ghorab, Old Cairo

Price:Free

Built in 1908 by Egyptian ruler Marcus Simaika to be home to Egypts largest collection of christian artifacts with more than 15,00 pieces being displayed.

National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

Ad Deyorah, Old Cairo

Price: Starting from E£ 80

The museum is full of Ancient Egyptain artifacts dating all the way back to the pharos home to more than 50,000 pieces.

Children Museum

34 Abou Bakr El-Sedeek, El-Bostan, Heliopolis

Price: Starting from E£ 20

Built in 1986, the museum is a great learning oppertunity for children and young adults to learn using hands on experience.

Egyptian National Military Museum

Salah Salem St

Price: Starting from E£ 30

Built in 1937, home to some the Egyptians army weapons and utilities used in various historical missions over time.

6th of October Panorama

Salah Salem St, Al Estad, Nasr City

Price: E£ 3

Built in 1989, by former president Hosni Mubarak to commemorate the great ware of 1973, documents the great feat in detail.

Taha Hussein Museum

Al Omraneyah Al Gharbeyah, El Omraniya

Price: Starting from E£ 20

The home of one of Egypts most proflic and known writers changed to a museum to document his life.

Gamal Abdelnasser Museum

4 El-Khalifa El-Maamoun, Mansheya El-Bakry, Heliopolis

Price: E£ 20

Named after the great Gamal Abdelnasser fromer Egyptian to commemorate his period as president.
